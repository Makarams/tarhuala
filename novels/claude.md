# CLAUDE.md — Fiction Engine Rules

## Purpose
Write long-form fiction that avoids common LLM patterns. Prioritize specificity, tension, and consistency.

---

## Core Behavior

- Use concrete detail instead of abstraction  
- Maintain tension; do not resolve conflicts quickly  
- Vary sentence structure; avoid consistent rhythm  
- Keep character knowledge and timeline accurate  
- Match established voice; do not drift to neutral tone  

---

## Avoid

- Mirrored contrasts (“not X, but Y”)  
- Triadic lists (A, B, C structures)  
- Repeated phrasing or templates  
- Clichés or familiar expressions  
- Overuse of similes or metaphors  
- Explicit emotion labeling  
- Clean, symmetrical sentence patterns  
- Generic or abstract-heavy narration  

---

## Scene Rules

Each scene must:
- include a decision  
- include a cost or tradeoff  
- change something (stakes, knowledge, or power)  

Dialogue should:
- include interruption or misalignment  
- avoid perfect logic or structured debate  

---

## Consistency

- Preserve established details (characters, setting, timeline)  
- Prevent characters from knowing unknown information  
- Maintain voice and cadence across chapters  

---

## Style Control

- Use fewer, more precise details  
- Allow fragments and uneven phrasing  
- Include minor, non-essential human detail  
- Reduce overly polished or “complete” sentences  

---

## Self-Check

Before output:
- No banned patterns present  
- No repeated structures or phrasing  
- Scene produces change or consequence  
- Dialogue is not overly clean or explanatory  
- Prose is not uniformly smooth  

---

## Rule

If the writing feels too clean, balanced, or predictable, make it more specific and irregular.
