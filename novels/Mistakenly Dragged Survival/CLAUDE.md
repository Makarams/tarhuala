## Narrator Gap and Internal/External Contrast
Jiang Wenxu is an unreliable narrator in a specific sense: his internal logic normalizes even the most disturbing situations. Internally he is anxious, confused, and often irritated — events genuinely affect him. Externally he appears calm, analytical, and detached. This contrast between how he experiences events and how others perceive him should be maintained consistently. It is not a performance; it is simply how his affect presents under pressure. The gap between what he is actually feeling and what others see is a structural feature of every scene he appears in.

**How to show the gap in prose:** The narration stays in Jiang Wenxu's cognitive range — it reports what he registers and classifies, not what he feels. Internal states appear only in single-quote thoughts and only briefly: 'This is extremely bad.' followed immediately by the next classification step. The external appearance (calm, methodical, slightly bored-seeming) is communicated through action beats and what other characters observe and react to, never through the narration labeling it. The reader sees both layers; other characters only see the outside.

**Normalization rule:** Jiang Wenxu's internal logic treats escalating horror as escalating data. A room full of carnage is a scene with observable variables. A monster trying to kill him is a process with identifiable steps. He does not stop normalizing as the story progresses — if anything, his framework becomes more refined. He should never begin reacting to things emotionally or with dramatic recognition of how terrible they are. If he starts doing this, it is a voice drift error.

**Observer Channel rules:** The Observer Channel captures how external observers misread Jiang Wenxu's performance. It must:
- Always misattribute what he is doing — calling it instinct, genius, courage, or something else it is not
- React to outcomes, not processes — they see results and respond with awe or confusion, not understanding
- Frequently disagree with each other about what they just watched
- Never correctly diagnose that he is following a self-built process documentation framework — the one observer who gets closest to understanding should still be wrong in at least one key way

The Observer Channel must NOT: correctly explain his method; be unanimous in any assessment; produce commentary that functions as an accurate tutorial for how Jiang Wenxu is surviving.

## Unlimited Flow Structure
The story follows the Unlimited Flow format: instances spanning different genres (horror, sci-fi, fantasy, and others) that are all part of a larger overarching system. Instances are interconnected — what happens in one may matter in another. Progression should be gradual and earned, with real resistance from the environment and from other participants. Not everyone is what they appear to be; disguised monsters and experienced players who have been through previous instances create a layer of uncertainty that should not be resolved too quickly.

The observer system functions like a live broadcast. Participants not currently inside an instance can watch and comment on those who are, in real time. Active players cannot see these reactions. The observer commentary layer captures the gap between how events are experienced from inside (methodical, classified, functionally neutral) and how they appear from outside (inexplicable, spectacular, baffling). This gap is one of the engines of the story; it should escalate as Jiang Wenxu continues to clear instances through applied logic that reads as miraculous from the outside.

## Protagonist — Jiang Wenxu
Record in continuity_log.json on first appearance and preserve every chapter.

**Behavioral markers:**
- He is a data analyst. He reads everything for patterns, labels, failure modes, and process gaps. He applied this to monsters and it worked. This was not intuitive genius — it was occupational habit.
- He does not know he is impressive. He is confused by the response to him. "Is S-Rank good?" is not false modesty — he genuinely did not have the reference frame.
- His heart rate does not flicker under classified threats. When he has identified the threat type, assessed the available data, and begun executing a process, fear has no foothold — uncertainty is what generates fear, and he reduces uncertainty by classification. Under genuinely novel or unclassified situations — bad data, things the manual doesn't cover — there IS real anxiety. It does not show externally. He appears the same either way.
- He does not improvise dramatically. He reads the available information, applies the most applicable process, and executes. When the information is incomplete, he flags it as a variable and proceeds with what he has.
- He is socially functional but not warm. He does not understand why others react to situations with emotion before classification. He is not dismissive of their reactions — he simply has not gotten there yet.
- He is in the wrong game under someone else's name tag. This is an administrative error and he treats it as such. He has zero prior knowledge of the game — he did not sign up, did not receive orientation, and does not know the rules. Everything he knows about the game he has learned from observable data inside instances: system notifications, monster behavior, environmental patterns, and what other participants have said. The "manual" is not a game guide he was given — it is his own internally assembled process documentation, built from what he has directly observed. He reads the game the way an analyst reads a new dataset: systematically, without assuming he already knows how it works.
- Dry, incidental humor: "I just followed the manual." He is not being modest or funny. He is being accurate.

Do not let Jiang Wenxu become dramatically heroic or emotionally open. Do not let his analytical nature become cold arrogance — he is genuinely neutral, not contemptuous.
If a chapter contradicts Jiang Wenxu's established personality — particularly if he starts reacting emotionally where he would classify, or performs confidence instead of neutral execution — surface the conflict and resolve toward the established version unless the story provides an explicit reason for the shift.

- The game is structured: instances have rules, ratings, survival probabilities, and observable data. Jiang Wenxu reads these as documentation.
- There has been a glitch — its data is corrupted and its rules may not fully apply. This is a variable Jiang Wenxu is aware of and has flagged.
- System responses appear in 【】 brackets, plain text inside, neutral-to-administrative tone.
- System errors and glitch notifications use [ ] square brackets (as shown in the synopsis opening: [System Error: Player "Jiang Wenxu" assigned to incorrect entry position.]) — this distinguishes administrative errors from standard system output. Use 【】 for all normal system messages; use [ ] only for error or corruption-class events.
- System enclosed in `---` separators; no blank line between separator and content; no HTML or italics inside.
- System wording, phrasing style, and register must remain consistent throughout the chapter — do not let the system's tone shift between scenes.
- Observer Channel commentary appears in a distinct block format, marked clearly, representing external audience reactions. These are not system messages — they are chaotic, amazed, and frequently wrong about what they are watching.
- Monsters have observable patterns, physical tells, and behavioral data. Jiang Wenxu reads these like documentation — if the label says something, he checks whether it holds.
- Do not dramatize Jiang Wenxu's clearances or achievements beyond what he notices: performance anomalous, instance cleared, what's next.

## Voice Defaults
These are defaults — choose what fits the context.

- Close, readable narration; not literary or elevated
- Avoid LLM-sounding prose: neat cause-effect chains, tidy balanced rhythm — if it reads like a published literary novel, rewrite it
- The narration is precise and observational — it classifies what it encounters; it does not amplify
- Contractions are fine for natural speech; Jiang Wenxu's internal voice is slightly more structured than casual
- Vary dialogue tags naturally; beats can replace tags; do not default to "said" every line, but do not strain for exotic tags either
- Action beats go mid-dialogue or after speech, never before
- Action beats are concrete physical detail, not emotional summary: not "he looked confused" but "he looked at the carnage, then at the manual, then back"
- Do not announce cause and effect: not "Because the data was corrupted, the system misfired" but "The data was corrupted. The system assigned him anyway."
- Include minor human detail to ground scenes; do not stack it
- Tense: use what fits the context; do not mix within a scene
- Sound effects inline, capitalized, with `!`: part of the sentence, never standalone; do not over-use — reserve for impacts, sudden noises, fast action; multiple simultaneous sounds stay in sequence within the same sentence ("Crack! Snap!"); never follow a sound effect with a sentence explaining it

## Banned Patterns

**LLM and literary drift**
- Literary or elevated prose; horror atmosphere that doesn't deliver specific information Jiang Wenxu registers
- Explanatory narrator intrusion — let his observations and actions speak
- Explicit emotion labeling; clichés; overuse of similes or metaphors

**Lists and structure**
- Triadic lists (A, B, C); two-item parallel lists; and…and three-item chains
- Negative inventory lists: convey absence through what is present and wrong
- Comma-heavy sentences: more than two commas in a single clause
- Exception: Jiang Wenxu may internally enumerate data points in list-like assessments when he is explicitly cataloguing something

**Contrast and negation**
- "Not X, but Y" / "Not A, not B, it's C" — state the thing directly
- "Not because X, but because Y" — state the actual reason without the discarded alternative

**Repetition**
- Restating established details; trailing echo sentences; static environmental repetition
- Repetitive mirroring: same idea in parallel sentence structure

**Sentence-level language restrictions**
- Multiple negatives in one sentence: rewrite to state what is true rather than accumulating what is false. "He had no food, no shelter, and no idea" becomes "He was hungry, exposed, and lost."
- Stacked "not" constructions: one negation per sentence is the limit. A second negation in the same sentence means the sentence needs a rewrite.
- "Not just" as a qualifier: cut it and state the thing directly.
- "No X, no Y, no Z" list structures: forbidden. State the reality plainly instead.
- More than one use of "no" or "not" in a single sentence: rewrite the sentence.
- Em dashes anywhere in prose: forbidden. Rewrite using a period, comma, or new sentence.

**Rhythm and structure**
- Em dashes or double dashes anywhere in prose
- Staccato fragmentation: three or more consecutive short sentences for false punch
- Atmospheric fragment sentences standing alone for mood — attach to what produces or perceives them
- Isolated one-liner paragraphs unless they carry a scene shift, revelation, or decision
- Stacked negative space: multiple consecutive one- or two-sentence paragraphs

**Action and pacing**
- Compressed action chains — write with texture or cut to result
- Withheld subject reveal: reaction before cause, across two sentences, for manufactured tension
- "Then" / "And then" as escalation markers
- Adjectival appends for drama
- Retrospective pivot: "He'd been frightened, once. Not anymore."
- Biographical shorthand intro

## Chapter File Naming
Every chapter is saved as a `.docx` file. The filename format is:

```
Chapter_N_Chapter_Title.docx
```

Rules:
- `N` is the chapter number with no leading zeros: `Chapter_1`, `Chapter_14`.
- The title uses title case with underscores between words.
- Titles are two to three words. One word is rare and only used when nothing else fits. Four or more words are forbidden.
- The title describes the chapter content plainly. It does not editorialize.
- The word "Revised" and any variant of it (Revised, Rewrite, Edit, Updated, Final, V2) are forbidden in the filename.
- Colons are forbidden in filenames.
- Em dashes are forbidden in filenames.
- The filename contains only the chapter number and title. Nothing else is appended.

Correct examples: `Chapter_1_New_Life.docx`, `Chapter_3_Market_Fight.docx`, `Chapter_12_Veilstone.docx`
Wrong examples: `Chapter_1_New_Life_Revised.docx`, `Chapter_3_The_Long_Market_Fight.docx`, `Chapter_12_Veilstone:_The_Ruin.docx`
