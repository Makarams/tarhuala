Nevertheless, looking ahead, there's a possibility she might repurchase it if she amasses enough coins in the future. For the time being, all she could do was patiently await a satisfying bid.

There were six options, but for now, her favorite was potions.


Scrolling through the list of potions, Lucy spotted some she liked, but their effectiveness in treating a bullet wound didn't seem promising. While there were names related to healing and pain tolerance, none of them implied they could heal a bullet wound. She could now read the descriptions and usage requirements, and still, none of them seemed to meet her needs.


Suddenly, a notification holographic screen popped up in front of her.


---


“You have received a bid of 10,001!”


“Do you confirm?”


---


‘1? Seriously?’ Lucy pondered. She had no choice but to decline. After all, she had initially set the starting price at 10,000, the standard rate for rare rank items, with the aim of generating more bids. A mere increase of 1 was far from her preference. Nevertheless, this process would require some patience.


It was currently three in the morning, and the sun would rise soon. Lucy wondered what Lucas was doing.


Turning to Belle, she said, “Belle, open the door. I need to talk to John.”


Time waited for no one, but so did her auction. She could only wait for time to pass, knowing that Lucas's health was slowly deteriorating.


Lucy knew she could open the door herself, but she was wary. Trust issues resurfaced due to her multiple bad encounters with other groups of humans. This situation could be considered one as well, as they had almost been killed. She didn't know if it was a good or bad thing that they only had three people who could fight. On the one hand, there were fewer to protect, like Cale. On the other hand, having more people might have helped in the earlier fight, but it also increased the chances of casualties in a bigger fight. Lucy didn't want to witness that.


Emerging from the black building, Lucy was greeted by the night sky. Although it was still dark, the air was fresh, and being on the rooftop brought a chilly breeze that was rather pleasant. She shifted her gaze and spotted John seated on the ground, leaning against the building's outer wall.


“John,” she called out.


Startled, John scrambled to his feet upon hearing her voice. “Yes?” he replied, still somewhat bewildered. Despite being older, everyone here understood that, for the time being, Lucy held authority over him.


“Do you have any items?” Lucy inquired.


“Items? Yes, I do have some,” John answered. It was expected; that if these people could use the auction building, it meant they had a way to earn coins, and that could only be achieved by using the system's items.


“Lead me to them,” Lucy instructed.


“No need,” John replied.


Thinking he was refusing; Lucy drew her katana and pressed it against his neck.


“Wait, wait, put that away. It's all on me,” John quickly clarified. He had initially said there was no need because the items were already on his person, and yet he had nearly lost his life due to a misunderstanding. ‘I have to choose my words carefully now,’ he thought.


Understanding his situation, Lucy withdrew her katana from his neck. Notifications of bidding could still be heard, but Lucy simply clicked “no” repeatedly; none of the offers met her demands.


“Show me,” she demanded.


John looked at her, his voice carrying a note of helplessness. “I can't use my hands. Can you untie me?”


“No,” Lucy replied firmly. She turned her gaze to Belle, who was fidgeting with her bag nervously. Lucy instructed her, “Belle will take them from you. Just tell her where they are.”


John sighed in resignation. “I have four of them on me; my ring, my belt, my shirt, and the pin in my pocket.”


“Do the others have items, too?” Lucy inquired.


“Yes, but mostly weapons, since they usually entrusted me with the items. I had to keep this place safe alone, but unfortunately, everyone else died.”


Nodding in response, Lucy planned to search the corpses for the items. She gestured to Belle and said, “Belle, take them off, John.”


Belle could only nod in understanding as she muttered, “Sorry, Uncle John.”
