They went back into the city that evening with robes on and weapons drawn, and found the bodies of two survivors in the first block.

Both were on the ground floor of a convenience store. Male, mid-twenties, system-players based on the gear — one had a knife with a glowing orange handle that showed Epic rarity when Lucas appraised it from two meters away, the other had what looked like a modified baton. Both had been overwhelmed. The convenience store's back door was open and a trail of green blood led out into the alley.

"Players," Lucy said.

"Yes." He looked at the baton. Useful weapon, gone to waste. He picked it up, appraised it — Uncommon grade, nothing special — and put it in the pouch anyway. The knife he left. Taking weapons off dead players felt different from taking weapons off dead zombies. He wasn't sure why that logic held but it did.

He checked the alley briefly and didn't go further. They had their own route planned.

He'd been thinking about the zombie hierarchy since the overpass that morning. Level 1 zombies reacted to noise and proximity. Level 2 zombies tracked, directed, and could suppress their own noise. Level 3s — he hadn't encountered a confirmed Level 3 yet, but the system's EXP values implied they existed and were worth 150 EXP each. What did a Level 3 do that a Level 2 didn't?

The answer came around the first corner of the second block.

It came from above.

He heard it before he saw it — a sound he'd never heard from a zombie, a short sharp percussive click, like someone snapping their fingers very close to his ear. He was already turning when the Level 3 zombie hit Lucy from the fire escape.

It had been clinging to the underside of the second-floor landing. It dropped three meters and landed on its feet directly behind her.

Lucy spun by instinct. The thing was fast — faster than the Level 2s, faster than anything he'd seen — and it caught the flat of her katana on its forearm rather than taking the cut. She staggered back against the wall. Lucas got between them and activated the Fire Ring. Fire Blaze — a three-second burst of fire that hit the zombie in the face at close range.

The zombie made a sound that wasn't human or animal, just the sound of burning and aggression, and kept coming.

He swung the jian. Crack! Into the collarbone, not a killing blow but it knocked the zombie sideways. Lucy drove the katana through its neck from behind. It dropped.

Both of them were breathing hard against the wall.

"It was hiding on the fire escape," Lucy said. Her voice was steady but she was holding the katana with both hands.

"I know." He looked up. The fire escape above was empty now. "They climb. Level 3s can climb and they know how to be quiet until they're overhead."

"How did you know it was Level 3?"

"I didn't." He looked at the dead zombie. It had been faster than anything else he'd killed, and the Fire Blaze hadn't stopped it immediately the way it stopped Level 1s. "That's how I know now."

She looked up at the fire escapes lining the alley. He could see her doing the math — building after building, three and four floors up, fire escapes on every one of them, and if any had Level 3 zombies waiting above them every block became a ceiling ambush.

"How many might there be?" she asked.

"No idea." He looked at the block ahead and then back at the way they'd come. "We move differently now. Watch overhead. Stay away from fire escapes."

She nodded once, tightly.

They went on.

The next three hours were brutal and effective. Nine more kills, two Level 2s and seven Level 1s, working through the first three blocks east of the main boulevard. The Level 2s were worth 100 EXP each and both times they spotted them first, which meant both times they had the angle. Lucy was Level 8 by the time they paused for water near the back of a bakery that still smelled of yeast and old bread.

He was at Level 11.

He'd put the 2 new stat points into intelligence again, and the system showed a slight change in how the Fire Blaze performed — he couldn't quantify it, but the flame had a longer reach now. Class abilities scaled with the Int stat. He filed that and drank water.

"I want to try the icicle in a real fight," Lucy said.

"You haven't?"

"I wanted to understand the range first. I practiced on the wall." She looked at the earring in the reflection of a dark window. "I think the throw range is about three meters."

"Try it on the next Level 2."

She did, ten minutes later. The zombie was standing alone in a side street and she hit it clean through the eye from four meters, not three. It dropped without making a sound.

"Four meters," she said.

"Good."

They worked for another hour after that. By the time they reached the edge of what felt like a reasonable risk — too many zombies converging on a crossroads two blocks ahead, drawn by the sounds of the earlier fights — they had both leveled again. He was at Level 12. She was at Level 9.

They retreated back through the cleared blocks and made it to the white building just after ten at night. He locked the door and sat down on the floor with his back against the wall and his hands in his lap, and for a moment he didn't do anything. Just sat there.

Lucy dropped beside him. She had a cut on her arm from the Level 3 — a shallow scrape, not deep — and she pressed the back of her hand against it to stop it bleeding.

"We did good," she said.

He looked at his hands. A small shake that he'd gotten in the habit of watching for, the kind that came after the adrenaline and before the tiredness. It was fading.

"We did," he agreed.

He pulled out the can opener and two cans of food from the pouch, and they ate in the quiet building while the city went dark outside.
