He put the appraisal magnifier to use properly for the first time on the morning of the sixth day.

He'd been carrying it since the class change building and using it in a basic way — pointing it at items to get grade and ability readouts. But the magnifier did more than that. He discovered this by accident when he held it up to Lucy's earring and the system gave him a detailed breakdown he hadn't seen before: ability cooldowns, exact range values, duration timers for effects.

He appraised everything.

He started with his own inventory, pulling items from the pouch one by one. The Fire Ring's Fire Blaze: burns target for 30 seconds, 2 uses per day. Fire Shield: creates a short-range protective fire barrier, 30 seconds, 2 uses per day. Hurricane Ring's Hurricane Spiral: violent horizontal wind burst, 5-meter radius, 30-second duration, 1 use per day. Hurricane Leap: wind-assisted jump, 3 times normal height, 3 uses per day. The Zombie Resistance Vambrace: protects the wearer from zombie infection on contact, 3 charges total. His own now read 3/3 still — he hadn't used any yet. The merged vambrace from Lucy's class change achievement still showed 1/3.

He stacked two cans of food for Lucy and ate his own can while he worked through the list.

"Your inventory is full of things you haven't appraised," Lucy said, watching from the floor.

"I know."

"That seems dangerous."

He didn't respond to that. She was right. He'd been carrying the Hurricane Ring for three days and had used Hurricane Leap twice to test it, but hadn't read the full cooldown timer or the exact effect radius for the Spiral. Now he knew. He adjusted his mental model accordingly.

He appraised the Steel Jian last. Rare grade, weight 1.1 kilograms, balanced for one-handed use. No special abilities — it was just a good sword with no system power attached. He'd been treating it like a better version of the dagger, which was roughly accurate. He put it back in his belt.

"My earring," Lucy said. "Icicle. Did you see the range?"

"Four meters. You figured that out last night."

"The system says three point five meters." She looked at the wall where she'd been practicing. "I hit at four."

"Your Agility increased since you acquired it. The stat probably affects throw range."

She thought about that. "So my effective range grows as I level up."

"Probably."

She picked up her Snow Robe and held it against her shoulders, checking the fit. She'd been wearing it in the evenings when it cooled down and taking it off during the day fights. He'd done the same with the Fire Robe — too warm to fight in, useful as armor when he wasn't moving fast.

"Tell me about the achievement," he said.

"Which one?"

"Yesterday. You leveled 8 and 9 both. There should have been a Platinum somewhere in there."

She checked her system and then looked at him. "How did you know?"

"The timing. You'd killed enough Level 2s."

She read it. "Platinum: Eliminate Four Level 2 and Forty Level 1 Zombies in Four Days. Reward—" She stopped.

"What's the reward?"

She looked at the object that had materialized on the floor beside her. A ring, the same kind of item style as his Hurricane Ring, but different coloring. And an item he hadn't seen before — a small lens in a copper frame.

She picked up the ring. "Hurricane Ring," she said.

"Same as mine?"

"Same name. Different coloring." She appraised it with his magnifier. "Same abilities. Hurricane Leap and Spiral." She looked at him. "I get three leaps a day now?"

"You had no leaps before."

She put the ring on her right hand, turned it. "What's the other item?"

"Appraisal Magnifier. Uncommon."

"You already have one."

"So now you have one."

She appraised the lens. The readout matched his — same grade, same function. She looked between the two magnifiers. "Two is redundant."

"Not entirely. If one breaks." He paused. "Keep it."

She put it in her jacket pocket. She looked at her achievement screen more carefully. "I also have one I didn't notice yesterday. Bronze: 88th to Discover the Class Change Building — the katana I already got."

"That one was always there."

"I know. I mean this other one." She read it. "Silver: Class Change in One Week. Reward: Zombie Resistance Vambrace 1/3 Rare." She looked at the vambrace that had appeared on the floor.

He looked at his own. 3/3 from the original class building achievement, 1/3 from the same Silver achievement Lucy had just triggered. He had four total charges across two items.

"What does it do, exactly?" Lucy asked.

"Blocks zombie infection." He held his own up. "If a zombie bites through my clothes, this activates and prevents the infection. Three charges, then it's done."

She put hers on her right forearm. "How does a zombie bite usually work? Does the infection spread fast?"

"I don't know. I haven't been bitten." He looked at her with the Vambrace on. "Don't test it."

"I wasn't planning to."

He finished his can and put it in the pouch to deal with later. He had 30 minutes before they needed to move — he wanted to be back in the city before the day got hotter, before the afternoon heat made fighting in robes completely impractical.

He looked at Lucy's full system display. Level 9, 440 EXP toward Level 10. She needed another 560 EXP today to change class. That was roughly twelve Level 1 kills, or six Level 2s. He had 860 EXP to Level 13. He'd probably level twice today if the density held.

He pulled his jian and stood up.

"Let's go," he said.

She was already on her feet.
